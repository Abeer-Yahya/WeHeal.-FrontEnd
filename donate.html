Rana Al Majali

Survivor Advocacy Lawyer

“One of the most empowering and therapeutic tools we have to eliminate stigma is telling and sharing stories. For many years, society has depended on victim blaming and silencing to cover up serious problems. Every story that is told erodes the protection that has been put up for far too long. Thank you so much for providing a secure space where people can speak up for their own healing as well as for all the other survivors out there who are feeling alone.”

Dana Al Nabulsi

PhD, Social psychiatry

“My 6-year career has been spent listening to survivor stories. The frequency with which I hear "You are the first person I have ever told" when these stories are mentioned continues to amaze me. My eyes are watering just thinking about those things, and I want to shout, "Please don't back away! Nothing you did was wrong. You deserve to be loved, cared for, and healed. I am also aware of the strength of sharing those tales. It lessens our sense of isolation, dispels the humiliation, and enables others to show us the respect and love we deserve. Finally, studies demonstrate that even writing about our experiences—here or in a journal—changes how we perceive things and leads to healing. Many thanks (We Heal.)”
